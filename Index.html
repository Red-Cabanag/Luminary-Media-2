OBS STUDIO

Why we chose OBS Studio as the software for our stream:

Description of Chosen Broadcasting Software: OBS Studio is a free, open-source software designed for video recording and live streaming, widely used by streamers and content creators for its powerful features and flexibility. It is compatible with Windows, macOS, and Linux, and supports high-quality streaming and recording, including HD and 4K video with minimal lag. OBS allows users to create and switch between customizable scenes, such as webcam feeds, gameplay, and intermissions, while incorporating various sources like video, images, and text. It offers advanced audio controls, including noise suppression and gain, and integrates seamlessly with major streaming platforms like Twitch, YouTube, and Facebook Live. Additionally, OBS supports third-party plugins and extensions, and provides hotkeys for real-time control of scenes and audio. Completely free and open-source, OBS offers professional-level tools without the cost or watermarks, making it a reliable choice for streamers looking for a customizable and stable solution.

Why we chose this software:

  • Free & Open-Source: No cost and customizable
  • Cross-Platform: Works on Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Advanced Features: Scene transitions, filters, and real-time source management
  • Large Community: Plenty of tutorials and support
  • Low Resource Use: Runs efficiently even on lower-end computers

Equipment that would be used for the stream:

  • Camera – A webcam or DSLR for high-quality video capture. Needed for clear, professional-looking video
  • Microphone – A high-quality microphone for clear audio. Essential for good sound quality, especially for voice overs or commentary
  • Audio Interface (Optional) – If using an XLR microphone, an interface is necessary for better sound quality and control
  • Lighting – Proper lighting equipment to ensure the subject is well-lit, preventing shadows and grainy video
  • Headphones – Over-ear headphones to monitor audio levels in real-time and prevent echo or feedback
  • Green Screen (Optional) – A green screen for virtual backgrounds and improved stream production value
  • Streaming PC – A powerful computer to run the streaming software smoothly, encode video, and handle multitasking
  • Capture Card (If Streaming from Console) – A capture card is needed to bring video from gaming consoles or external cameras into the streaming software
